Past Theses¶

The entries below summarize completed student theses built around CTRL-P workflows and related additive-manufacturing control problems.

Student Level Project Summary Thesis
Emir Marengo BSc Beat the AI Compared zig-zag and spiral heuristics against the CTRL-P RL agent for 2D toolpath filling under Manhattan constraints. The RL agent was most efficient, but the spiral heuristic remained rule-consistent and substantially more reliable when the agent filled non-target cells or failed outright. Open thesis
Jos Gartsman MSc Adaptive Nozzle-Speed Control for 3D Concrete Printing Using Reinforcement Learning Built parametric and FEM-based 3D concrete printing models and trained RL agents to adapt nozzle speed while staying close to buckling limits. The FEM-backed controller reached slightly higher printable heights and the agents remained responsive under variable curing rates, showing how RL can balance productivity and structural stability. Open thesis
